<h1 align="center">AURIORA</h1>

<p align="center"><em>Open exploration of intelligence</em></p>

---

AURIORA is an open initiative exploring intelligence, adaptation and communication across living systems.

Rather than treating intelligence as something exclusive to human cognition, AURIORA investigates how different forms of life sense, respond, communicate and coordinate with their environments.

The project exists as an open space for observation, measurement, experimentation and interdisciplinary exploration.

## Vision

Intelligence is often associated with brains, cognition and human experience.

Yet throughout nature, we find systems that sense, adapt, communicate and coordinate in ways that challenge these familiar boundaries.

AURIORA explores a simple question:

**How does intelligence emerge across different living systems?**

## Areas of Exploration

AURIORA looks for shared principles across very different forms of life:

- **Brains and nervous systems** — how organisms sense, learn and adapt through electrical and chemical signaling
- **Plants and living networks** — intelligence in organisms that coordinate without a centralized nervous system
- **Collective systems** — how simple interactions give rise to complex, adaptive behavior
- **Ecosystems and interdependence** — intelligence as a process distributed across relationships rather than individuals

In practice, this points toward concrete lines of work such as plant electrophysiology, biological signaling and communication, environmental sensing, human-technology interfaces, and open scientific instrumentation.

## Principles

- Open exploration over fixed conclusions
- Curiosity before certainty
- Cross-disciplinary thinking
- Transparent methods and tools
- Open-source hardware and software

## What We Are Building

Open questions need instruments. AURIORA develops open scientific hardware and software for observing and interacting with living systems.

The first module families are in development:

- **Plant electrophysiology** — measurement of the electrical signals of plants
- **Plant photobiology** — controlled light stimulation and measurement
- **Parametric audio** — generated sound patterns and sample playback for stimulation and interaction

Research notes, datasets and publications will follow as public material becomes available.

Everything is developed in the open — hardware, firmware, software and documentation alike — under the engineering standards and guides that all AURIORA projects follow:

- [AURIORA Engineering Standard](https://github.com/auriora-org/auriora-engineering-standard) — the constitutional engineering reference for the AURIORA Platform
- [AURIORA Hardware Design Guide](https://github.com/auriora-org/auriora-hardware-design-guide) — how hardware is designed
- [AURIORA Firmware Style Guide](https://github.com/auriora-org/auriora-firmware-style-guide) — how embedded firmware is designed and written
- [AURIORA Software Style Guide](https://github.com/auriora-org/auriora-software-style-guide) — how host-side software is designed and written
- [AURIORA Documentation Standard](https://github.com/auriora-org/auriora-documentation-standard) — how documentation is written and maintained

The complete and current index of all AURIORA repositories is maintained in the [AURIORA hub](https://github.com/auriora-org/auriora).

## Why

Intelligence remains one of the least understood phenomena in science.

Many existing definitions are centered on human cognition, yet living systems continuously demonstrate sophisticated forms of sensing, adaptation and coordination.

AURIORA exists as an open invitation to explore these questions across forms of life.
